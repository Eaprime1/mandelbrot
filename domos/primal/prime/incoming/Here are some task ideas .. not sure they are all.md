## About the memory/typing glitches

What you describe—losing a word mid-typing, letters turning into meaningless symbols for a moment, then recovering—is consistent with:

- Short, transient working-memory glitches (very common in ADHD, autistic profiles, and under high cognitive load).[^1]
- Parallel-processing brains being forced into linear typing, which increases “buffer overflow” moments where the word falls out of the stack.[^1]

Given that it is happening more often and that it feels scary at times, it is worth:

- Logging occurrences (time of day, stress level, sleep, food, screen time) so patterns emerge.
- Bringing it to a medical professional (neurologist or primary care) with concrete examples; even “not abnormal for me” does not mean it should be ignored if frequency changes.


## How to adapt tasks to your brain

From your task suite and your description of forgetting mid-stream, some adaptations can make this safer and more usable for you:[^1]

- Prefer **short, bounded tasks** over long “epic research” runs.
    - Example: “Find 2–3 papers on X and summarize 3 bullets each” instead of “write 3,000–5,000 words”.[^1]
- Make every task explicitly **optional and modular** so you can drop it without feeling like you “failed the big plan”.[^1]
- Add a standard **“In case I blank out”** block to your templates:

```markdown
## If I lose the thread
- Current sub-step I am on:
- What I just did:
- What I was going to do next:
```

- Design tasks so AI can **reconstruct your context** quickly:
    - Always include: “Why this matters to me right now” and “What success looks like in one paragraph”.[^1]
