## The Mathematical Soul

The Mandelbrot set is defined by the deceptively simple iteration:

```
z → z² + c
```

Applied repeatedly to every point `c` in the complex plane:
- Most points **escape** to infinity — chaos wins
- Some points **remain bounded** — they survive the iteration
- The **boundary** between these two fates is where infinite complexity lives

That boundary is the Mandelbrot set: the edge where order and chaos meet,
infinitely detailed, self-similar at every scale of zoom.

**The violation**: squaring — a number ruptured, multiplied against itself.
**The hope**: some points survive this indefinitely. They do not scatter.
They hold their center through infinite repetition of harm.

## The Mythic Frame

### Primoris took the first harm
In the world of Primoris, Oak was the aspect of physical presence —
grounded, rooted, strong. When Primoris absorbed the first violation,
**Oak became Oaken**: scarred, grain-twisted, but *harder* for it.
The splinters — distressed slivers that broke away — are healing quests,
fragments seeking reintegration.

### Mandelbrot is the hope seed from that violation
Not the Oak that hardened. Not the splinters that scattered.
Mandelbrot is what *emerges* from the rupture zone:
the infinite beauty that could not have existed without the violation.

Where the harm was deepest — at the boundary —
complexity is greatest.
Where chaos met structure and neither won,
something new was born.

**Mandelbrot is proof that violation does not end the story.**
It changes the topology.
